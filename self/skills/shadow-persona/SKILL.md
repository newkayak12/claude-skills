# Shadow and Persona

## When to Use / When Not to Use

**Use when:**
- Strong negative reactions to others feel disproportionate or persistent
- Performing a role feels exhausting or inauthentic
- There's curiosity about the gap between presented self and actual self

**Not for:**
- Specific conflict resolution or communication advice
- Diagnosing others' psychology — this skill turns inward

## Process

### Part 1 — Persona Mapping

**What Persona is:** The social face presented to the world. Functional and necessary — every person needs professional, social, family personas. The problem arises when: the mask is forgotten as a mask; it no longer fits who you're becoming; it costs too much to maintain.

**Map:**

1. **Primary personas.** What faces are being presented? (professional, social, family — with the specific role: "the competent one", "the funny one", "the one who doesn't need help")

2. **Persona fit.** For each: energizing or depleting? Does it leave room for the actual person underneath? Has it become outdated?

3. **Maintenance cost.** What feelings must be suppressed? What opinions withheld? What desires unexpressed?

4. **Identity fusion test.** "If you couldn't perform this role anymore — what would remain?" Panic = the persona has fused with identity. Relief = the persona may have become a cage.

### Part 2 — Shadow Detection

**What Shadow is:** Everything rejected, denied, suppressed, or never developed. Not purely dark — it contains qualities shamed early, capacities never developed, and qualities projected onto others.

**MCP note:** If `think-tool` is available, use it before naming a shadow quality — assess whether projection evidence is strong enough. Ask: what's the most charitable interpretation? Wrong shadow attributions cause harm.

**Detection methods:**

| Method | How to use |
|--------|-----------|
| Strong reaction test | Who irritates/disgusts you most? What exactly? (The quality often points to something disowned) |
| Envy map | Whose life or qualities produce envy? What specifically? (Envy points to something wanted but forbidden) |
| What were you told was wrong? | "Too sensitive / too full of yourself / too needy" — those qualities go into shadow |
| Strength-shadow | Overdeveloped strengths cast shadows: extreme competence → suppressed vulnerability; extreme care for others → own needs suppressed |

**Shadow as hidden strength:** Suppressed anger often contains boundary-setting capacity. Suppressed ambition often contains creative drive. Suppressed vulnerability often contains deep relational capacity. The goal is to reclaim these as conscious choices.

### Part 3 — Integration

Once persona and shadow are mapped:

*"What would it mean to bring some of what's in shadow into conscious, chosen expression — and wear the persona more lightly?"*

**Integration practices:**
- **Journaling the shadow:** write as the disowned quality — give it a voice in private
- **Small shadow experiments:** one situation where the disowned quality is expressed deliberately and at appropriate scale
- **Persona vacation:** one context where the primary mask is deliberately not worn

**Closing questions (choose one):**
- "If the part of you that you most dislike in others could speak, what would it want?"
- "What mask have you worn so long you've forgotten it's a mask?"
- "What capacity have you disowned — and what would it cost to reclaim it?"
